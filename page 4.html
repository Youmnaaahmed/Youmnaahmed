Awareness Programs: Although this level does not require direct disability services, the association may focus on awareness programs to prevent injuries that could lead to mobility impairment.

Sports Activities: Encouraging participation in sports activities to maintain physical fitness and prevent diseases.

Rehabilitation and Physiotherapy: Programs to provide physiotherapy sessions to help individuals improve their mobility and maintain fitness.

Daily Skills Training: Teaching skills that help in dealing with minor difficulties in daily life.

Psychological and Social Support: Providing psychological counseling and support groups to help individuals adapt to their disability.

Assistive Devices: Providing or facilitating access to assistive devices such as crutches, wheelchairs, or other devices that assist in mobility.

Comprehensive Rehabilitation: Comprehensive rehabilitation programs that include physical therapy and occupational therapy to improve performance in daily activities.

Transportation Services: Providing transportation services or facilitating access to public transportation designed for people with disabilities.

Personal Care Services: Providing personal care aids at home to help individuals with daily activities such as dressing and bathing.

Vocational Rehabilitation: Training and rehabilitation programs that help individuals acquire new skills or modify the jobs they can perform based on their abilities.

Legal Support: Helping individuals obtain their legal rights and financial assistance.

Social and recreational activities: Organizing social, sports and cultural events to promote social integration and reduce isolation.

Training programs: Providing training opportunities for families and community members on how to support people with motor disabilities.

All these services aim to improve the quality of life of people with motor disabilities and enable them to live with the greatest possible independence and dignity.
